Synopsis
Manufacturer of machines used to perform inspections of integrated circuit devices brought action against competitor, alleging infringement of patent disclosing method for using incident laser radiation to measure the coplanarity of a substrate. The United States District Court for the Central District of California, Lourdes G. Baird, J., found patent to be valid and infringed. Competitor appealed. The Court of Appeals, Gajarsa, Circuit Judge, held that: (1) patent encompassed measurement method in which index pads could either be separately fabricated or simply selected from existing signal pads, and (2) patent was not invalid for obviousness.
 
Affirmed.
