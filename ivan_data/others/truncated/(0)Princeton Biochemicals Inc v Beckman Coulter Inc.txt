DECIDED: June 9, 2005.
Synopsis
Background: Owner of patent for capillary electrophoresis device sued competitor for infringement. The United States District Court for the District of New Jersey, Mary L. Cooper, J., 2004 WL 1398227, found patent invalid as obvious, and owner appealed.
 
The Court of Appeals, Rader, Circuit Judge, held that invention was combination of known elements that would have been obvious to one of ordinary skill in art at time of invention.
 
Affirmed.
