Synopsis
Holder of three patents for balloon dilation catheters used in coronary angioplasty procedures brought patent infringement suit against competitor. The United States District Court for the Northern District of California, Martin J. Jenkins, J., 1999 WL 398003,granted summary judgment to competitor. Patent holder appealed. The Court of Appeals, Bryson, Circuit Judge, held that: (1) common specification in patents which referred to an inflation lumen "separate from" guide wire lumen disclaimed competitor's catheter with dual lumens configuration, and (2) patents were not infringed under doctrine of equivalents.
 
Affirmed.
 
Dyk, Circuit Judge, concurred and filed opinion.
