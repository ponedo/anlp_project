Synopsis
Background: Owner of patents for use of decorative assemblies to hold flower arrangements sued competitor for infringement. The United States District Court for the Southern District of Illinois, Michael J. Reagan, J., 316 F.Supp.2d 693, held patents valid and infringed, but found no induced or contributory infringement. Parties cross-appealed.
 
The Court of Appeals, Dyk, Circuit Judge, held that patents were invalid as anticipated.
 
Reversed.
